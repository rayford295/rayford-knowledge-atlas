## Summary

This article studies freight truck crash severity in Los Angeles through the lens of spatial inequity and counterfactual inference.

## Method Snapshot

The paper uses counterfactual inference to reason about crash severity and uneven risk exposure. It gives the atlas a transportation-safety branch connected to urban fairness, not only model performance.

## Impact

This output helps connect GeoAI and urban analytics to public accountability. It asks where risk concentrates, who bears that risk, and how spatial evidence can make those patterns harder to ignore.
