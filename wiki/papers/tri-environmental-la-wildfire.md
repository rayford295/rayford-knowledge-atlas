## Summary

This article evaluates the economic exposure of the 2025 Los Angeles wildfire through a tri-environmental framework. It brings natural, built, and social environmental data into one daily wildfire impact assessment.

## Method Snapshot

The workflow combines VIIRS daily fire detections, OpenStreetMap built-environment data, and high-resolution dasymetric population modeling. The study estimates exposure across social, built, and natural environments at fine spatial and temporal resolution.

## Data and Study Area

The case study focuses on the Eaton and Palisades wildfire regions in Los Angeles County. The analysis tracks January 2025 wildfire progression and estimates potential economic exposure of about $48.55 billion.

## How This Connects to My Other Work

This paper connects the disaster branch of the atlas to earth observation and exposure modeling. It complements street-view damage assessment by showing how satellite fire detections and open geospatial data can support regional impact evaluation.

## Impact

The work adds a public-facing wildfire and remote-sensing node to the research graph. It strengthens the atlas narrative around disaster resilience, spatial intelligence, and equitable emergency planning.
